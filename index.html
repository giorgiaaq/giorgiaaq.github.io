Welcome to the exploration of the architectural heritage left by Frederick II in the regions of Sicily and Apulia.

Frederick II, Holy Roman Emperor, significantly influenced Italian architecture by constructing numerous castles across Sicily and Apulia. His castles are characterized by their unique blend of Norman, Arab, and Byzantine styles. These fortifications exemplify his innovative approach to military architecture and his desire to reflect cultural diversity and imperial power.

Frederick II's castles are remarkable examples of medieval architecture. Their distinctive architectural style is known as Swabian Style.
